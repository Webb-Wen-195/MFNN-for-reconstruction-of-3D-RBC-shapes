# MFNN-for-reconstruction-of-3D-RBC-shapes
A multi-fidelity neural network (MFNN) for reconstructing 3D red blood cell morphology from partial 2D cross-sections. Fuses high-fidelity cross-sections with a similar low-fidelity reference shape, regularized by surface-area and volume constraints.

This repository accompanies the paper "Reconstruction of three-dimensional shapes of normal and disease-related erythrocytes from partial observations using multi-fidelity neural networks" by Haizhou Wen, He Li, and Zhen Li. (
https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2511.14962)
We propose a MFNN approach that fuses high-fidelity 2D cross-sections of a red blood cell (RBC) with a morphologically similar low-fidelity reference 3D RBC shape to recover the full 3D surface. The predictor combines a convolutional neural network (trained on low-fidelity reference data) with a feedforward neural network that captures nonlinear morphological correlations, and augments training with surface-area and volume constraints for regularization. The method is grounded in a topological homeomorphism between a sphere and 3D RBC surfaces, with training data generated by dissipative particle dynamics (DPD) simulations of the stomatocyte–discocyte–echinocyte transformation.
Benchmarked across diverse normal and aged RBC shapes, the MFNN reconstructs complex morphologies with over 95% coordinate accuracy from as few as two orthogonal cross-sections. The workflow also includes an image-extraction step enabling direct 3D reconstruction from experimentally acquired 2D microscope images.
